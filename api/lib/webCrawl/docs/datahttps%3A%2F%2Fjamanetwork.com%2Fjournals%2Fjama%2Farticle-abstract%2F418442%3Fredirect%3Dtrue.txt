Its use in dementias is based on positive results from only a few controlled clinical trials, most of which did not include standard assessments of cognition and behavior.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;h3&gt;Objective.&lt;/h3&gt;&lt;p&gt;—To assess the efficacy and safety of EGb in Alzheimer disease and multi-infarct dementia.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;h3&gt;Design.&lt;/h3&gt;&lt;p&gt;—A 52-week, randomized double-blind, placebo-controlled, parallelgroup, multicenter study.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;h3&gt;Patients.&lt;/h3&gt;&lt;p&gt;—Mildly to severely demented outpatients with Alzheimer disease or multi-infarct dementia, without other significant medical conditions.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;h3&gt;Intervention.&lt;/h3&gt;&lt;p&gt;—Patients assigned randomly to treatment with EGb (120 mg/d) or placebo. Safety, compliance, and drug dispensation were monitored every 3 months with complete outcome evaluation at 12, 26, and 52 weeks.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;h3&gt;Primary Outcome Measures.&lt;/h3&gt;&lt;p&gt;—Alzheimer&#39;s Disease Assessment Scale—Cognitive subscale (ADAS-Cog), Geriatric Evaluation by Relative&#39;s Rating Instrument (GERRI), and Clinical Global Impression of Change (CGIC).&lt;/p&gt;&lt;h3&gt;Results.&lt;/h3&gt;&lt;p&gt;—From 309 patients included in an intent-to-treat analysis, 202 provided evaluable data for the 52-week end point analysis. In the intent-to-treat analysis, the EGb group had an ADAS-Cog score 1.4 points betterthan the placebo group (&lt;i&gt;P&lt;/i&gt;=.04) and a GERRI score 0.14 points better than the placebo group (&lt;i&gt;P&lt;/i&gt;=.004). The same patterns were observed with the evaluable data set in which 27% of patients treated with EGb achieved at least a 4-point improvement on the ADAS-Cog, compared with 14% taking placebo (&lt;i&gt;P&lt;/i&gt;=.005); on the GERRI, 37% were considered improved with EGb, compared with 23% taking placebo (&lt;i&gt;P&lt;/i&gt;=.003). No difference was seen in the CGIC. Regarding the safety profile of EGb, no significant differences compared with placebo were observed in the number of patients reporting adverse events or in the incidence and severity of these events.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;h3&gt;Conclusions.&lt;/h3&gt;&lt;p&gt;—EGb was safe and appears capable of stabilizing and, in a substantial number of cases, improving the cognitive performance and the social functioning of demented patients for 6 months to 1 year.     <span>—EGb 761 is a particular extract of Ginkgo biloba used in Europe to alleviate symptoms associated with numerous cognitive disorders. Its use in dementias is based on positive results from only a few controlled clinical trials, most of which did not include standard assessments of cognition and behavior.</span></p><p><strong>Objective.</strong> 
    <span>—To assess the efficacy and safety of EGb in Alzheimer disease and multi-infarct dementia.</span></p><p><strong>Design.</strong> 
    <span>—A 52-week, randomized double-blind, placebo-controlled, parallelgroup, multicenter study.</span></p><p><strong>Patients.</strong> 
    <span>—Mildly to severely demented outpatients with Alzheimer disease or multi-infarct dementia, without other significant medical conditions.</span></p><p><strong>Intervention.</strong> 
    <span>—Patients assigned randomly to treatment with EGb (120 mg/d) or placebo. Safety, compliance, and drug dispensation were monitored every 3 months with complete outcome evaluation at 12, 26, and 52 weeks.</span></p><p><strong>Primary Outcome Measures.</strong> 
    <span>—Alzheimer's Disease Assessment Scale—Cognitive subscale (ADAS-Cog), Geriatric Evaluation by Relative's Rating Instrument (GERRI), and Clinical Global Impression of Change (CGIC).</span></p><p><strong>Results.</strong> 
    <span>—From 309 patients included in an intent-to-treat analysis, 202 provided evaluable data for the 52-week end point analysis. In the intent-to-treat analysis, the EGb group had an ADAS-Cog score 1.4 points betterthan the placebo group (<i>P</i>=.04) and a GERRI score 0.14 points better than the placebo group (<i>P</i>=.004). The same patterns were observed with the evaluable data set in which 27% of patients treated with EGb achieved at least a 4-point improvement on the ADAS-Cog, compared with 14% taking placebo (<i>P</i>=.005); on the GERRI, 37% were considered improved with EGb, compared with 23% taking placebo (<i>P</i>=.003). No difference was seen in the CGIC. Regarding the safety profile of EGb, no significant differences compared with placebo were observed in the number of patients reporting adverse events or in the incidence and severity of these events.</span></p><p><strong>Conclusions.</strong> 
    <span>—EGb was safe and appears capable of stabilizing and, in a substantial number of cases, improving the cognitive performance and the social functioning of demented patients for 6 months to 1 year. Although modest, the changes induced by EGb were objectively measured by the ADAS-Cog and were of sufficient magnitude to be recognized by the caregivers in the GERRI.</span></p>            <div class="movable-ad-target bta"> </div>

                if (typeof string === 'string') {
                    return string.toLowerCase() === 'true';
                }

                return undefined;
            }

            // Get viewport dimensions w/o the need for Modernizr or some other dependency
            function updateViewportDimensions() {
                var w = window, d = document, e = d.documentElement, g = d.getElementsByTagName('body')[0], x = w.innerWidth || e.clientWidth || g.clientWidth, y = w.innerHeight || e.clientHeight || g.clientHeight;
                return { width: x, height: y };
            }

            // setting the viewport width
            var viewport = updateViewportDimensions();

            // Debounce function. Use this on resize to avoid taxing the browser.
            var debounce = function (func, wait, immediate) {
                var timeout;
                return function () {
                    var context = this, args = arguments;
                    var later = function () {
                        timeout = null;
                        if (!immediate) func.apply(context, args);
                    };
                    var callNow = immediate && !timeout;
                    clearTimeout(timeout);
                    timeout = setTimeout(later, wait);
                    if (callNow) func.apply(context, args);
                };
            };

            // --------------------
            // variables we can use throughout the application. e.g. if (App.isMobile), etc.
            (function (App) {
                // breakpoint variables
                App.mobileUpperBreakpoint = 767;
                App.mobileAltBreakpoint = 640;
                App.tabletUpperBreakpoint = 1023;

                function setVariables() {
                    // variables that are dependent on viewport
                    viewport = updateViewportDimensions();
                    App.isMobile = (viewport.width <= App.mobileUpperBreakpoint);
                    App.isMobileAlt = (viewport.width <= App.mobileAltBreakpoint);
                    App.isTablet = (viewport.width > App.mobileUpperBreakpoint && viewport.width <= App.tabletUpperBreakpoint);
                    App.isTabletDown = (viewport.width <= App.tabletUpperBreakpoint);
                    App.isDesktop = (viewport.width > App.tabletUpperBreakpoint);
                    App.activeViewport = App.isMobile ? 'small' : App.isTablet ? 'med' : 'large';
                }

                setVariables();

                // other variables that are useful throughout the app
                // The "pg_" value corresponds to the class name found in the aspx.cs file
                // (i.e., "pg_learningsearchresults" corresponds to "public partial class LearningSearchResults...").
                App.cachedViewport = App.isMobile ? 'small' : App.isTablet ? 'med' : 'large';
                App.isAnyArticle = !!(scmByClass('widget-ArticleFullView').length || scmByClass('pg_abstract').length || scmByClass('widget-MagazineView').length);
                App.isSplitScreen = !!scmByClass('widget-ArticleFullView').length;
                App.isAbstract = !!scmByClass('pg_abstract').length;
                App.isMag = !!scmByClass('widget-MagazineView').length;
                App.isFreeArticle = !!(scmquery('.widget-ArticleTopInfo .free-access') ||
                                        scmquery('.widget-ArticleTopInfo .open-access') ||
                                        scmquery('.widget-ArticleTopInfo .icon-free'));
                App.isIssue = !!scmByClass('pg_issue').length || !!scmByClass('pg_newonline').length || !!scmByClass('pg_currentissue').length || !!scmByClass('pg_pastissues').length;
                App.isNewOnline = !!scmByClass('pg_newonline').length;
                App.isCurrentIssue = !!scmByClass('pg_currentissue').length;
                App.isPastIssues = !!scmByClass('pg_pastissues').length;
                App.isJournal = !!scmByClass('pg_journal').length;
                App.isNetworkHome = !!scmByClass('pg_index').length;
                App.isSearch = !!scmByClass('pg_searchresults').length;
                App.isAdvancedSearch = !!scmByClass('pg_advancedsearch').length;
                App.isPlayer= !!scmByClass('pg_multimediaplayer').length;
                App.isSelfServe = !!scmByClass('pg_sspage').length;
                App.isMyAccount = !!scmByClass('pg_myaccount').length;
                App.isLearning = !!scmByClass('pg_learning').length;
                App.isLearningContent = !!scmByClass('pg_learning').length || !!scmByClass('pg_learningsearchresults').length || !!scmByClass('pg_quiz').length || !!scmByClass('pg_multimediaplayer').length || !!scmByClass('pg_journalarticlecme').length;

                // Because we use one aspx page to display all self serve content, we really don't have a good way of
                // determining whether we showing learning SS or not so we need to check the pathname.
                App.isLearningSelfServe = window.location.pathname.indexOf("/learning/pages/") >= 0;

                App.isLearningSearchResults = !!scmByClass('pg_learningsearchresults').length;
                App.isQuiz = !!scmByClass('pg_quiz').length;
                App.supportsFlexbox = !!scmByClass('flexbox').length;
                App.isCollection = !!scmByClass('pg_collection').length;
                App.isJournalArticleCme = !!scmByClass('pg_journalarticlecme').length;
                App.isPaidUser = isTrueString(scmById('hfAppIsPaidUser').getAttribute('value'));
                App.isMember = isTrueString(scmById('hfAppIsMember').getAttribute('value'));
                App.isIndividual = isTrueString(scmById('hfAppIsIndividual').getAttribute('value'));
                App.hasAccess = isTrueString(scmById('hfAppHasAccess').getAttribute('value'));
                App.isReferredFromGoogle = isTrueString(scmById('hfAppIsReferredFromGoogle').getAttribute('value'));
                var subscribedTo = scmById('hfAppSubscribedTo').getAttribute('value');
                App.userSubscribedTo = subscribedTo ? subscribedTo.split(',') : undefined;
                App.isLucy = isTrueString(scmById('hfAppIsLucy').getAttribute('value'));
                App.currentJournalCount = 13;

                App.updateAppVariables = debounce(function () {
                    setVariables();
                }, 50, false);
            })(App);
        </script>
